Climate Impacts

Understanding how our changing climate affects the world's most precious travel destinations

Increased Temperatures and Heat Stress

Ghana is experiencing rising temperatures, with projections indicating further increases. This leads to increased heat stress, impacting human health (especially vulnerable populations like the elderly and children), labor productivity in manual work (like agriculture and mining).

Sea-Level Rise and Coastal Erosion

Ghana's coastline is experiencing a significant rise in sea levels, leading to increased coastal erosion. This threatens coastal communities, infrastructure, agricultural land (due to salinization), and ecosystems, forcing some residents to relocate.

Changes in Rainfall Patterns and Water Scarcity

Rainfall patterns are becoming erratic, with predictions of more intense downpours leading to floods, alongside longer dry periods and droughts. This variability affects water security for drinking, sanitation, and hydropower production (which accounts for a significant portion of Ghana's electricity).

Impacts on Agriculture and Food Security

A large portion of Ghana's workforce depends on rain-fed agriculture, making it highly vulnerable to climate change. Rising temperatures and erratic rainfall reduce crop yields (e.g., maize, cassava), increase pest attacks, and lead to land degradation and desertification.

Health Impacts

Climate change exacerbates various health issues in Ghana. This includes an increase in vector-borne diseases (like malaria and dengue fever) due to altered water conditions, and waterborne diseases (like cholera and diarrheal diseases) linked to flooding.

Extreme Weather

Both inland and coastal areas are highly susceptible to flooding. Intense and erratic rainfall leads to riverine and urban flooding, while sea-level rise exacerbates coastal flooding and erosion. Floods cause displacement, damage to infrastructure (homes, roads, schools), disruption of livelihoods.

Sustainable Travel Tips

Choose Eco-Friendly Accommodations

Look for hotels or lodges with green certifications. If staying at someone's home, try to choose places that use renewable energy and have eco-friendly practices.

Support Local Business

Buy from local vendors and artisans. Eat at locally-owned restaurants. Choose locally-run tour companies. This supports the local economy and reduces environmental impact.

Stay at Green Hotels

Look for eco-certified hotels that use renewable energy, have water conservation programs, and recycle. Many hotels now have green initiatives to reduce their environmental impact.

Conserve Resources

Take shorter showers, reuse towels, turn off lights and air conditioning when not in the room. Be mindful of water and energy consumption during your stay.

Pack Light & Smart

A lighter luggage means less fuel consumption for transportation. Pack versatile clothing that can be mixed and matched. Bring only what you need to minimize waste.

Choose Direct Flights

Reduce emissions by booking direct flights when possible. Takeoffs and landings use the most fuel, so fewer connections mean lower carbon footprint. Consider train or bus for shorter distances.

Offset Your Carbon Footprint

Invest in verified carbon offset programs. Many airlines offer carbon offset options. You can also support renewable energy projects or reforestation initiatives to balance your travel emissions.

Pack Reusable Items

Pack reusable items like water bottles, shopping bags, and utensils to reduce plastic waste. Bring your own toiletries in refillable containers and avoid single-use items.
